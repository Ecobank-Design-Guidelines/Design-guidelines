Vision

Our aim is to have a single site where new and existing clients can browse and manage all their financial requirements, securely. This site will have tailored content and will promote consistent brand engagement.

The goal is to, firstly, reduce the friction that users currently experience when interacting with the brand and, secondly, to allow us to deliver a superior product that meets the user’s every need.

Our vision is our North Star, as a collective we align to it to ensure that we deliver the best experience for our customers.

Know who you’re designing for

Discover

We look at how users behave so that we can learn what they want and understand what their needs are. It is essential for us to see things from the user’s eyes so that we can ensure that we share the same vision and buy-in before we start defining our design hypothesis.

Create

By exploring many design solutions, and refining them, we are able to focus our design efforts and craft our design hypothesis.

Validate

By measuring our design solutions against our hypothesis we can validate our design thinking. This approach allows us to make mistakes early and cheaply and ensures very low risk of customer rejection.

Build

Bring the solution design to reality. Testing plays a critical part of ensuring the feature is successful as it transactions into product.

Deliver a consistent, unified experience

To create a rich and unified experience for the customer regardless of where their journey begins. Use consistent language, patterns, and components across the interface to make it easier for our squads to get stuff done.

Design with modules

We’ve started to breakdown our interfaces into reusable modules and started using those patterns to build products, features, and interfaces quicker than ever before.

The benefits of a modular approach are that:

• it’s cost-effective (modules can be reused);
• it’s relatively easy to maintain (you can amend one module without affecting the others); and
• It’s adaptable (modules can be assembled in different ways to meet different user needs).

Build the right product for the right people

When thinking of creating experiences that make the user’s life easier, we often think of a simple, beautiful, and easy to use feature-set of a product. But the core experience is not a set of features, it’s the job the users hire the product for.

It fulfils a need or solves a problem people have. If the problem exists and the design solution fits the hypothesis, the product becomes meaningful to our customers and they will start using it.
