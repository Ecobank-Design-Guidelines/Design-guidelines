Accessibility



Our design accessibility enables users with disability to understand, interact and navigate on our platforms.


Accessibility defines users’ ability to use our platforms, being that there are a number of people living with disabilities.

Color blindness

This documentation references internationally accepted accessibility principles, standards and guidelines to assist designers and developers in ensuring accessibility of applications and digital content.

• vision impairment: users who employ screen readers and screen magnification software - 7.6% of South Africans have a mild difficulty in seeing, and 1.3% have severe difficulty in seeing. The proportion of South Africans who have no difficulty in seeing decreases as age increases (from 97% at age 5–9 years to 51% at age 85+ years). • hearing impairment: deaf or hard-of-hearing users who may need audio transcripts or sign language interpretation - 3% of South Africans, that are 5 years and older, have mild difficulty in hearing, and 1% have severe difficulty in hearing; • intellectual and cognitive disabilities: users with conditions such as dyslexia, ADD, ADHD, etc. - 3.2% of South Africans have mild difficulty remembering or concentrating, and 1% having severe difficulty, and; • physical disabilities: users who browse via the keyboard, or who use a mouth stick, eye tracking software, voice recognition, cheek movement tracking, etc. - 2.5% of South Africans have mild difficulty in walking, while 1% reported having severe difficulty in walking a kilometre or climbing a flight of stairs.
